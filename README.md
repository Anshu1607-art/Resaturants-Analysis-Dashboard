# Zomato Restaurants Analysis Dashboard

An interactive and comprehensive **Power BI Dashboard** that visualizes international restaurant distributions, consumer engagement metrics, and food ordering habits across global markets. 

##  Project Overview
This project transforms unstructured food-aggregating global transaction data into an enterprise-level decision tool. Key focus areas include cross-border presence mapping, cuisine popularity testing, and feature-adoption ratios (such as table booking vs. online delivery options).

The report features an exhaustive multi-level filter pane allowing seamless regional slicing by **Country** and timeline tracking across historic operational years (**2010 to 2018**).

---

##  Key Operational Metrics Monitored

* **Total Restaurants Listed:** Macro KPI measuring the vendor catalog scale (spanning 9.55K+ establishments globally).
* **Geographical Reach:** Instant tracking of business footprints across multiple distinct Cities (141) and Countries (15).
* **Average Ratings:** Consolidated food service performance indexing (averaging 2.89 stars across global markets).
* **Aggregate Customer Votes:** Public engagement metric tracing feedback traction (accumulating over 1.82M+ unique consumer reviews).
* **Cuisine Diversity Count:** Catalog tracking showing active listings across 1.82K+ distinct cuisine classifications.

---

##  Tech Stack & Data Architecture

* **Tool:** Power BI Desktop
* **Data Modeling:** Designed clean transactional logic mapping distinct descriptive attributes to spatial fields (e.g., matching coordinates to designated Cities/Countries).
* **DAX & Metrics Design:** Implemented dedicated measures calculating operational weights, average pricing scores, and relative rating splits across varied distribution segments.

## Key Insights from the Dashboard

*   **Global Footprint & Scaling:** The platform tracks a vast network of over 9.55K restaurants distributed heavily across core metropolitan regions, spanning 141 cities worldwide.
*   **Feature Adoption (Online vs. Offline):** A strong contrast exists between operational models, with **Online Delivery** options missing from a significant portion of listed vendors (73.41% non-participating), highlighting a massive untapped expansion frontier for active logistics conversion. 
*   **Booking Preferences:** Table bookings remain an exclusive boutique luxury feature, with only a small fragment (12.12%) of international restaurants providing reserved booking spaces.
*   **Cuisine Specialization:** The data reveals a heavy focus on native comfort food varieties, where regional variations like North Indian, Mughlai, and Chinese hold the highest share of listings across major urban hubs.
*   **Quality vs. Volume Check:** While specific popular hubs maintain high restaurant listing volumes, the highest average customer satisfaction scores frequently correlate with specialized niche markets, rather than high-density urban clusters.
